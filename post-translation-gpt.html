Creating a Nuanced Translation GPT for Shia Texts

August 25, 2025 10 min read
Share Copy Link

Translating sacred texts is not a simple language task. It requires fidelity to meaning, correct terminology, and a reverent tone. I built a Translation GPT that focuses on Twelver Shia Islamic texts and respects these needs.

The challenge

Generic tools often miss theological nuance. They can drop honorifics or use terms that do not reflect Shia doctrine. That breaks trust and clarity.

Workflow design

  • Tool first extraction. Always extract text first. Use OCR when needed. Do not translate until the raw text is confirmed.
  • Persona and rules. The model is a master translator that uses precise terms and honorifics. It follows a glossary and a reference guide.
  • Line by line output. Preserve structure and argument flow. Use Markdown with clear headings and quotes.
  • Clarifications. Add short notes when a term is dense or culturally specific.

Quality practices

  • Include honorifics for the Prophet and the Imams with respect.
  • Use consistent English terms for Arabic and Persian concepts.
  • Avoid ambiguous phrasing that may shift meaning.

Results

The assistant produces translations that preserve meaning and tone across Gujarati, Urdu, Arabic, and Persian sources. It reduces errors and keeps a consistent style across long documents.

Takeaway

When the task is sensitive, rules matter more than speed. A simple protocol and a firm set of terms can raise trust and quality.
